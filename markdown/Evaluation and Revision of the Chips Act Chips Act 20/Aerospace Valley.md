Aerospace Valley is the largest European cluster in the aerospace sector, covering aeronautics, space, and drones with link with the defense sector regarding the duality of some technologies. It brings together more than 800 members, including major industrial companies, SMEs, startups, and research laboratories. The EUs dependence on chip supplies is well recognized. Funding is needed across both front-end and back-end activities, particularly for advanced silicon nodes and heterogeneous integration of technologies, to strengthen European technological sovereignty. The aerospace and defense industrieswhose systems increasingly demand higher computing power, enhanced security, and guaranteed integrity while reducing carbon footprinthave very specific requirements. These include reliability, lifespan, and radiation performance, all directly linked to their mission environments. Sovereignty is becoming a critical issue for maintaining leadership in aerospace and defense applications. Although production volumes in these sectors are relatively low, targeted Chips Act 2 funding is expected to address these specific chip characteristics and potentially integrate them as early as the design phase. At the same time, the EU PCB industry is facing significant challenges and should be considered within Chips Act 2, as it remains a weak link in the electronics value chain. In the near future, embedding active and passive components (packaged or not) within PCBs will become a key differentiator in terms of both functional performance and physical integration. Ensuring reliability will be essential for these advanced technologies and will require dedicated measures.
