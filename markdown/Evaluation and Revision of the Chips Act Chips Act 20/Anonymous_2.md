The EU Chips Act A Strategic Perspective for Europe As the open consultation phase for the EU Chips Act begins, it is crucial to reflect on where Europe should position itself in the global semiconductor landscape. While subsidies funded by taxpayers often dominate the discussion, I believe public money should not be used to reinforce markets already controlled by major global players. These segments are mature, highly consolidated, and offer limited room for Europe to create unique value. Instead, Europe should focus on profitable niches where it can leverage its strengths: Consensus-building and collaboration Europes ability to align diverse stakeholders is a competitive advantage. Specialized markets Targeting areas where fragmentation and innovation thrive, rather than competing head-on in commoditized sectors. Value creation for Europe Investments should prioritize technologies and ecosystems that strengthen European sovereignty and long-term competitiveness. The Chips Act should not be about replicating what others have already mastered. It should be about shaping a future where Europe leads in strategic, high-value segments that matter for its economy and society.
