Bavaria is one of Europes most important semiconductor regions, with a focus on chip design, and is also a strong user location. A revised version of the European Chips Act (ECA) is expressly welcomed; however, the following points should be taken into account: ECA 2.0 should address the entire value chainfrom materials and chip design (including tools for chip design) to the printed circuit board. The first of a kind criterion partly prevents an effective strengthening of resilience. In the interest of our user industries, building additional capacities for legacy chips is particularly important. Therefore, for investment projects of special strategic importance, transparent, operational criteria should be defined in place of first of a kind. Considering recent experiences with NEXPERIA, it is also proposed that the backend - especially Advanced Packaging - be given significantly higher priority within ECA 2.0. Given the strategic importance of this issue, it is recommended that the European Union provide substantially more funding in the next financial framework. However, the focus should not solely be on improving supply through the highest possible subsidies. It is also about strengthening the demand side so that producing the relevant chips in Europe becomes profitable for companies. Simply postulating Buy European or linking it to subsidies does not appear promising if consumers are not willing to accept the associated risk premium in the final price. One possible approach could be a substantial expansion of data center capacities. It is proposed that potential European user industries, which as consumers of semiconductors form the backbone of our industrial base and whose needs in some cases vary greatly, be explicitly included in the objectives of ECA 2.0. and, where appropriate, prioritized (funding rate, speed). Particular mention should be made of the security and defense industry, for which secure and trusted supply chains (trusted electronics) are of especially high importance. Even if production volumes are often lower, the defense policy relevance is enormous. In addition, a successful energy transition with sustainable expansion of renewable energy increasingly requires intelligent control through specialized chips for grids. SMEs and innovative Start-Ups should receive special support regarding funding levels and assistance with the highly complex funding procedures. At the same time, the speed of the application process is crucial for the investment planning of applicant companies. A fast-track procedure should therefore be established at the European Commission. Furthermore, it is requested that regions be explicitly involved in ECA 2.0, as they are in direct contact with relevant companies and often contribute significantly to financing through co-funding. Explicit reference is made here to the position paper of the European Semiconductor Regions Alliance (ESRA) from July 2025. The microelectronics sector suffers from a shortage of skilled workers, which, according to numerous studies, will intensify significantly in the coming years. It therefore seems sensible that quantitative and qualitative measures to ensure the skilled workforce become a dedicated pillar of the ECA. Given the complexity, ECA 2.0 cannot actively address all relevant areas. However, it is suggested that (institutionalized) interfaces with other policy fields - such as securing rare earth elements - be explicitly included.
