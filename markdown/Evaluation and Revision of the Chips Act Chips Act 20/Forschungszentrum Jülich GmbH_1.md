All AI activities are today based on the same technical principles represented in the usage of GPUs which is from system perspective very power consuming and inefficient and will only get some evolutionary improvements. For next genrations of AI the already in science existing new principles (including usage of emerging devices) need more attention - Neuromorphic Computing principles. These opens the capability of revolutinary new AI implementations with tremendously decreased power needs. But this also means a more specialized co-design effort form application and training over algorithms down to implementations and specific technologies. These activities are today driven out of the scientific communities and need to be represented in the future ChipsJU in a sufficent manner to enable European sovereignty . This means (without claiming completeness): 1) "easy and affordable" access to pilot lines with feature sizes below 10nm to make it economically interesting for companies 2) integration of "emerging devices" (e.g. memristors, ferroelectric transistors, etc.) into the pilot line processes 3) continous transfer capability of explorative devices into pilot lines 4) PDK development efforts for such devices 5) library design and provision of certain functionalties with these devices for pilot lines 6) improvement of development workflows for such devices 7) Development of co-design workflows All this should lead to more extensive, esp. also HW realted research and transfer on future AI principles. BTW, this is in no way in competetion to the Quantum Computing efforts inside EU and should be seen as complemetary with target to make use of the European strengths in make use of new concepts and not "brute force" scaling.
