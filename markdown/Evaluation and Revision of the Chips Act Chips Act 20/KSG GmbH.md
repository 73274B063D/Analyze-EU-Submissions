As a fully European PCB manufacturer, we want to emphasize a crucial point: Europe needs more than chips. The current Chips Act is an important first step, but it does not sufficiently strengthen Europes overall electronic and system-level resilience. By focusing mainly on front-end semiconductor manufacturing, the Act leaves critical bottlenecks in the back end of the electronics supply chain unresolvedthereby increasing Europes dependency on third countries. This poses concrete risks for defence, automotive, industrial automation, AI data centers, and other strategic sectors. Chips alone do not create functionality. They become operational only when integrated through conductive structures on printed circuit boards (PCBs) and assembled into PCBA modules and full systems. These layersPCB and PCBA production, packaging, and system integrationare strategic choke points. Addressing them is essential for maintaining Europes technological leadership and sovereignty. Additionally, Europe risks losing vital expertise. PCB technology is highly complex; deep competence cannot be developed through academic training alone but requires many years of industrial experience. PCBs and electronic assemblies are also susceptible to technical manipulation, as demonstrated in several studies (e.g., PANDA). Our recommendation is clear: Europe needs a Chips Act Plus that covers the entire electronics value chain. Specifically, we propose: Expanding the scope from silicon to complete systems: explicitly recognising PCB and PCBA manufacturing as strategic priorities, supported by dedicated funding instruments. Creating targeted incentives to strengthen European PCB and PCBA capacities, including minimum European content requirements and public support for building and maintaining production capabilities. Requiring PCBs that are manufactured and assembled in Europe for all safety- and security-critical applications and infrastructures not only to reduce strategic dependencies, but also to safeguard the essential industrial know-how that would otherwise be lost. In summary: Europe must take a holistic approachinvesting from chip fabs to end systems, from silicon and advanced packaging to PCB/PCBA manufacturingto secure technological resilience, sovereignty, and competitiveness. A Chips Act Plus is essential to achieve this.
