The European Chip Act is an important step and has already attracted investmens. Nevertheless, focusing solely on semiconductors will not reduce Europe´s dependence on Asia. Europe has just a small share for PCB´s used in Europe. Generally the share of Europe is below 2% worldwide. We urgently need to improve the resilience of our system, not only with regard to transmitted PCB data that could be misused for negative purposes, but also in terms of supply chain reliability. => We have to further invest into the entire supply chain - especially in PCB´s. In addition: - European PCB manufacturers pay high duties on essential base materials, which are not sufficiently produced in the EU. - Finished PCBs imported from China, however, are duty-free creating unfair competition. - Exempt base materials such as copper laminates, epoxy resin prepregs, and FR4 from customs duties. - Europe is investing in chips whereas the USA is investing in chips and additionally PCB - => Requirement of additional PCB investment Without targeted support for Europes PCB industry through customs relief, investment incentives, and IP protection the European Chip Act remains incomplete. Chips always need PCBs, and Europes security depends on a strong domestic PCB sector.
