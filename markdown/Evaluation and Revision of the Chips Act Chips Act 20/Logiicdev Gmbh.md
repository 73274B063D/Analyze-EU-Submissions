A. Learning from Global Best Practices The Taiwan Example To strengthen Europes semiconductor competitiveness, we should learn from successful models such as Taiwans ecosystem, which demonstrates: 1. A complete and integrated value chain where SMEs play a vital role across design, manufacturing, and supply. 2. A business-driven innovation model, in contrast to Europes predominantly research-driven approach. 3. Direct government support to SMEs for technology development, talent retention, and industrial partnerships with large enterprises. 4. A practical, business- and innovation-oriented education system, whereas many EU students remain focused on academic recognition and dissemination. 5. Cost and production efficiency achieved through streamlined processes unlike the EUs growing administrative burden, with excessive focus on reporting, timesheets, and bureaucracy. B. SME Support and Monitoring Funding schemes should ensure that fund are primarily used for employing and retaining skilled personnel, rather than being consumed by administrative tasks, reporting requirements, and multiple meetings. C. Access to Funding for SMEs University and RTO-led spinoff/projects often secure funding more easily, while SMEs face significant barriers in accessing support, even in chip-related initiatives. Targeted mechanisms should be established to ensure fair and equitable access for SMEs. D. Clear Focus in Chip-Driven Calls Future CHIPS JU and ECS calls should provide clearer guidance and prioritization for semiconductor-focused projects, particularly to enable new fabless companies and practical industrial outcomes. from last 2years they are overly generic calls & dilute the impact on Europes strategic semiconductor goals. E. Streamline Consultancy-Based Support Remove all consultancy based support/program. This middle man just do bla-bla and ask SME to do the job. instead they should support and do work with SME, offering hands-on technical assistance, co-development, and practical implementation support.Funding should prioritize active contribution over administrative mediation to maximize efficiency and impact. F. Consolidate Clusters and Networks Under a Unified Framework The existing landscape of multiple clusters, centers, and initiatives is highly fragmented, often involving high membership fees and regional bias, which excludes many SMEs. A single, transparent umbrella framework (like amazon.com for shopping) should be established to coordinate all semiconductor-related services, funding access, and networking activities. This would increase accountability, reduce duplication of effort, and ensure equitable access to all SMEssimilar to a unified digital platform model offering end-to-end services.
