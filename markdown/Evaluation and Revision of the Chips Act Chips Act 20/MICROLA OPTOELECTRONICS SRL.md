  investment in fundamental and applied research; transnational cooperation mechanisms;
  and a regulatory framework that enables sustainable, secure and competitive growth
  in the semiconductor sector. Only by converging these elements will the European
  Union be able to consolidate its technological autonomy, strengthen economic resilience
  and preserve digital sovereignty in the medium and long term. The semiconductor
  production chain is identified as follows: Materials, production tools Research
  and Development (R & D) and Design (design) and Intellectual Property (IP) Production
  (manufacturing) on wafers the so-called front-end assembly, packaging and testing
  back-end Distribution and supplies to end device manufacturers (OEMs). Apart from
  materials, where Europe is particularly unrich, all the sectors identified above
  should be targeted. Particular attention should be paid to sectors with competitive
  advantages, such as the design and manufacture of production machines and tests
  in order to maintain or increase their technological value for international trade.
  R & D and IP need further strengthening to reach a comparable Technology Readiness
  Level with the US. The wafer level production sector alone requires considerable
  economic efforts, well above the figures invested so far, and the production process
  is fully automated, requiring a small number of highly specialised skills that are
  now active in the manufacturing of production tools and machines. This sector can
  be relocated to socio-politically stable and energy friendly areas. The semiconductor
  back-end, which is currently particularly under development, requires scientific
  and technological investment to produce heterogeneous electronic circuits that can
  be integrated at die level and not with the classic PCB process. This innovative
  approach, which has long been taken by major international companies on mass production
  products, will have a significant advantage in sectors such as: defence, security,
  automotive and especially industrial automation including robotics.'
