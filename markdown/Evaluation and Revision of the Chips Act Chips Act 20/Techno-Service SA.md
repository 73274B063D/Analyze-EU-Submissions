TS PCB, a Polish manufacturer of Printed Circuit Boards (PCB), strongly supports the European Unions ambition, articulated within the European Chips Act, to bolster technological sovereignty, security, and resilience in the semiconductor supply chain. The pursuit of enhanced European manufacturing capacity is vital for economic security. However, we must raise a critical and specific concern regarding the scope of the Act: the exclusion of the Printed Circuit Board (PCB) manufacturing sector from its core strategic definitions and support mechanisms. A Technical and Legal Gap in Scope The current framework of the European Chips Act primarily channels support through measures defined under Pillar 2, focusing on the establishment of 'First-of-a-Kind' and 'Integrated Production Facilities (IPFs)' or 'Open EU Foundries (OEFs)'. This core legislative focus defines the scope of strategic support and priority too narrowly. Narrow Definition of 'Integration': By concentrating investment and regulatory efforts almost exclusively on semiconductor fabrication (wafer level) and advanced packaging technologies, the Act fails to account for the crucial interconnection layer necessary to make any chip functional. The PCB is the indispensable physical and electrical foundation that enables the integration of chips into any final electronic system. Exclusion of Critical Enablers: The PCB manufacturing sector, which provides the critical substrate and interconnection technology for all IPFs and OEFs to be practically operationalized, is effectively excluded from these strategic definitions. This creates a literal supply chain gap: we may secure the silicon chip, but if the substrate (the PCB) must still be sourced from vulnerable non-EU supply chains, the goal of true end-to-end resilience is not met. The Threat to Supply Chain Security Ignoring the PCB sector introduces a significant vulnerability that fundamentally undermines the Acts primary objective: Incomplete Resilience: The PCB becomes the unprotected critical bottleneck. Achieving high volumes of European-made chips provides no strategic advantage if their deployment is limited by the availability, quality, and security of corresponding European-made advanced PCB substrates (such as High-Density Interconnect - HDI, and complex multilayer boards). Technological Alignment: Investment in cutting-edge semiconductor nodes requires parallel investment in advanced PCB manufacturing to handle the high density and signal integrity requirements. A gap in the European PCB sector will directly inhibit the effective uptake and integration of the very advanced chips the Act aims to promote. Concrete Recommendation TS PCB urges the European Commission and the co-legislators to formally expand the definitions and scope of support under the European Chips Act 2 to explicitly include the Advanced Printed Circuit Board manufacturing industry. This expansion must ensure that: PCB manufacturing facilities producing advanced substrates are recognized as strategic, 'first-of-a-kind' enablers of the European electronics ecosystem. Funding and regulatory mechanisms are available to stimulate R&D and significant capacity expansion in complex, high-reliability PCB technologies within the EU. A secure European semiconductor supply chain is impossible without a secure, advanced European PCB supply chain. We appeal to the European Commission to close this critical technical and legislative gap.
